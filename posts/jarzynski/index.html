Importance sampling ⚖️⚖️ : The Jarzynski connection

March 2022

It is not easy to find a continuous dynamic XtX_tXt​, such that at each time ttt the distribution of XtX_tXt​ matches ptp_tpt​. One might be tempted to use the overderdamped Langevin dynamics,

dXt=−∇Vt(Xt)dt+2dWtdX_t = - \nabla V_t(X_t)dt + \sqrt{2}dW_t dXt​=−∇Vt​(Xt​)dt+2​dWt​

ρ˙t=Δρt−∇⋅[ρt∇Vt].\dot{\rho}_t = \Delta\rho_t - \nabla \cdot [\rho_t \nabla V_t].ρ˙​t​=Δρt​−∇⋅[ρt​∇Vt​].

If ρt\rho_tρt​ was equal to ptp_tpt​, then ptp_tpt​ should also satisfy this equation; but for ptp_tpt​ one easily checks that the RHS is zero, resulting in p˙t=0\dot{p}_t=0p˙​t​=0 which is false in general if we choose a family of evolving potentials; it would only be true at equilibrium when VtV_tVt​ is constant.

The Jarczynski trick allows to reconnect the distribution of XtX_tXt​ with ptp_tpt​. It introduces a weight, the solution of an auxiliary ODE, such that its conditional expectation given Xt=xX_t=xXt​=x gives pt(x)p_t(x)pt​(x), hence correcting the mismatch between ρt\rho_tρt​ and ptp_tpt​.

(In the sequel we note Ft=log⁡ZtF_t = \log Z_tFt​=logZt​, and the dot in V˙t\dot{V}_tV˙t​ or F˙t\dot{F}_tF˙t​ always represents a derivative with respect to time. )

Jarczynski's goal was to compute, at least numerically, the partition functions ZtZ_tZt​ or equivalently the free energy Ft=−log⁡ZtF_t = - \log Z_tFt​=−logZt​. The Jarczynski estimator, discovered in 1996, simply consists in applying (5) to φ=1\varphi=1φ=1, and noting that WtW_tWt​ is a simple ODE solved by

Wt=W0e−∫0tV˙s(Xs)−F˙sds=ZtZ0e−∫0tV˙s(Xs)ds.W_t = W_0e^{-\int_0^t \dot{V}_s(X_s) - \dot{F}_s ds} = \frac{Z_t}{Z_0}e^{-\int_0^t \dot{V}_s(X_s)ds}.Wt​=W0​e−∫0t​V˙s​(Xs​)−F˙s​ds=Z0​Zt​​e−∫0t​V˙s​(Xs​)ds.

Jarzynski's identity states that

E[e−∫0tV˙s(Xs)−F˙sds]=1.\mathbf{E}[e^{-\int_0^t \dot{V}_s(X_s) - \dot{F}_sds}] = 1. E[e−∫0t​V˙s​(Xs​)−F˙s​ds]=1.

The consequence of this identity is that

log⁡E[e−∫0tV˙s(Xs)ds]=Ft−F0, \log \mathbf{E}[e^{-\int_0^t \dot{V}_s(X_s)ds}] = F_t - F_0, logE[e−∫0t​V˙s​(Xs​)ds]=Ft​−F0​,

giving rise to a natural estimator for the free energy difference Ft−F0F_t-F_0Ft​−F0​: one samples many paths from the dynamics (2), then computes the path integral wt=∫0tV˙s(Xs)dsw_t = \int_0^t \dot{V}_s(X_s)dswt​=∫0t​V˙s​(Xs​)ds along each path, and then average the values of e−wte^{-w_t}e−wt​ and take the log.

In general, exponential of paths integrals are not easy to study. The classical way to prove Jarczynski's identity directly is to use the Feynman-Kac representation of PDEs, but this proof seems quite involved; instead, the trick above with the augmented system seems to be easier. It was shown to me by Eric Vanden-Eijnden.

Uniqueness of solutions to parabolic PDEs

We're now left with checking that the solutions of

Uniqueness for solutions of parabolic equations is well-known to PDE theorists and can be found in Evans' book, chapter 7. We provide a proof for completeness.
